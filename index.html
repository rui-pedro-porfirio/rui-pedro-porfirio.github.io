About

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at the NOVA School of Science and Technology. My research is centered in Human-Computer Interaction, particularly, in researching how making intelligent systems can impact the user experience of farm stakeholders in smart farming solutions.

My path to user-focused research started in the corporate world. Before my PhD, I was a Technology Consultant at Deloitte, where I worked in the development of large-scale software for the insurance industry. That experience was the catalyst for my shift into academia. It taught me that the human side of technology is just as important as the code. The leadership and agile project skills I developed there are also something I now use every day in my research and when mentoring students.

My Master's thesis was my first deep dive into academic research, and it confirmed my passion for this work. It became clear that my interests lie at the intersection of people and intelligent systems. Today, my work is grounded in Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) and more specifically, Human-AI Interaction (HAI). I focus on how Multimodal Systems and Explainable AI (XAI) can be combined to build experiences that are transparent and centered around the users' mental models.

Research Projects

My projects focus on the practical challenges of designing and evaluating intelligent systems from a human-centered perspective.

  • Explainable AI in Digital Agriculture Solutions

    As the core of my PhD thesis, this research focuses on researching how explainability influences the user experience of farm stakeholders when interacting with digital solutions. Through direct collaboration with the end users, we are gathering insights on how system transparency affects their perception, trust, and practical use of smart farming technologies.

  • AI-Driven Decision Support for Precision Vineyards

    As a research member on an Europen-funded project, I am contributing to the development of a digital platform that provides AI-driven decision support for vineyard management, by leveraging multimodal data such as remote satellite imagery and soil data. My work involves the integration of multimodal AI models and Explainable AI (XAI) to deliver transparent and actionable insights to vineyard stakeholders.

  • Indoor Positioning for Cross-Device Environments

    For my Master's thesis, I developed an indoor positioning system using Wi-Fi and BLE signals to manage device association in cross-device environments. The project involved applying machine learning models and fuzzy logic, and it resulted in a functional Proof-of-Concept web application and two peer-reviewed publications at international conferences.

Publications

My research has been published in peer-reviewed international conferences. Below is a selection of my work.

AgriUXE: Integrating Explainable AI and Multimodal Data for Smart Agriculture

2024 International Symposium on Sensing and Instrumentation in 5G and IoT Era (ISSI)

Rui Pedro Porfírio, Rui Neves Madeira, Pedro Albuquerque Santos
This paper introduces AgriUXE, a novel platform designed to provide user-adaptive explanations in smart farming by integrating XAI with multimodal data.

Enhancing Digital Agriculture with XAI: Case Studies on Tabular Data and Future Directions

2024 International Conference on Multimodal Interaction (ICMI Companion)

Rui Pedro Porfírio, Pedro Albuquerque Santos, Rui Neves Madeira
We explore the application of Explainable AI techniques on agricultural data through case studies on yield prediction.

Studying the Impact of Explainable AI in Digital Agriculture Solutions

2024 Computer-Supported Cooperative Work and Social Computing (CSCW Companion - Doctoral Consortium)

Rui Pedro Porfírio
This paper outlines my doctoral research plan to investigate how explainability can impact the user experience within digital agriculture solutions.

AI-powered Solution for Plant Disease Detection in Viticulture

2024 International Conference on Ambient Systems, Networks and Technologies (ANT)

Miguel Madeira, Rui Pedro Porfírio, Pedro Albuquerque Santos, Rui Neves Madeira
We introduce a mobile application designed for viticulture tasks. It incorporates remote sensing visualization, treatment customization, and decision support tools based on machine learning inference.

Computational Framework to Support Development of Applications Running on Multiple Co-located Devices

2021 ACM SIGCHI Symposium on Engineering Interactive Computing Systems (EICS Companion)

Pedro Albuquerque Santos, Rui Neves Madeira, Rui Pedro Porfírio, Nuno Correia
This work details a framework for generalizing the development of applications with user interfaces distributed across multiple co-located devices.

Indoor Positioning System for Ubiquitous Computing Environments

2021 Intelligent Data Engineering and Automated Learning (IDEAL)

Pedro Albuquerque Santos, Rui Pedro Porfírio, Rui Neves Madeira, Nuno Correia
We describe an indoor positioning system using Wi-Fi, BLE, and machine learning to enable proxemic interactions in ubiquitous computing.
